A Data Warehouse is a system that collects and integrates data from multiple sources into a single, consistent data store to support data analytics, AI, machine learning, OLAP, and business intelligence (BI) reporting.
Benefits:
Improves data quality and decision-making
Speeds up business insights
Helps organizations gain a competitive edge

Deployment Options:
On-premises
Cloud-based
Hybrid

Popular Vendors: Oracle, Teradata, Vertica, Google, IBM, Microsoft, Snowflake, Amazon
Key Evaluation Factors: Features, compatibility, ease of use, support, and total cost of ownership (TCO), including infrastructure, compute, storage, and maintenance costs.
Data Marts:A data mart is a smaller, focused part of a data warehouse designed for specific business functions. It typically uses a star or snowflake schema, contains clean and validated historical data, and can be dependent, independent, or hybrid in relation to the enterprise data warehouse (EDW).
IBM Db2 Warehouse:
Cloud-ready and highly scalable
Offers automated schema generation and built-in machine learning
Integrates with JDBC, Apache Spark, Python, and RStudio
